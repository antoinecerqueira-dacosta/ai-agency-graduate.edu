[ 01 ] The question

How does AI use shape the evolution of a graduate student's agency?

AI tools have moved from novelty to default. They draft emails, structure arguments, summarise readings, suggest the next thought before you've finished the previous one. The change is fast, and the questions worth asking are rarely loud ones.

This study follows a small group of graduate students for ten weeks. Not to prove a thesis or assign blame, but to listen carefully, through weekly self-documentation and three one-to-one conversations, to what people notice about themselves as their relationship with these tools settles into routine.

Where does your thinking end, and the model's begin? The question this study is built around.

[ 03 ] How it unfolds

A short weekly check-in. Three quiet conversations. That's it.

01

Week 1

Baseline survey & opening interview

You'll begin with a short context-setting survey and a 20-minute opening conversation. We talk about your background, the way you currently work, and what your relationship with AI looks like at the start.

02

Weeks 1-10

Weekly self-documentation

Each week, a short online survey with a small set of standardised questions and two open reflections. It takes 10–15 minutes. You complete it in your own time, in whichever language you prefer. The aim is to track quiet shifts: not to test you, not to grade you.

03

Week 5

Mid-point interview

A second 20-minute conversation, in person or via Microsoft Teams, to look at what's shifting and what isn't. There are no right answers, we're interested in your experience.

04

Week 10

Final conversation

A slightly longer closing interview (~25–30 min) to take stock of the term and look forward. Then the study closes, you receive a copy of the consent form, your data is pseudonymised, and the analysis begins.

Honesty over polish.

The most useful answer is rarely the most articulate one. We ask you to be candid about how you actually work: messy patterns, half-formed thoughts, things you'd hesitate to say in a seminar. The study only works if participants feel safe enough to say what they mean.

You can decline any question, withdraw at any moment, or take a week off if you need to. You keep your own data identifier (a 5-digit code only you know) so that withdrawal and rectification stay possible throughout.

[ 04 ] Common questions

Before you say yes.

Will my professors or the school see my answers? +
No. Responses are pseudonymised by design and accessible only to the two researchers. Nothing you say in the survey or interview is shared with faculty, peers, or administrators.
Do I need to be a heavy AI user to take part? +
Not at all. The study is interested in the full range of relationships people have with these tools, including avoidance, ambivalence, and skepticism. Light users and non-users contribute as much to the picture as enthusiasts.
Can I withdraw mid-study? +
Yes, at any point and without explanation. You can also request that the data collected up to that point be deleted, provided your responses are still technically identifiable via your personal code.
Will I be recorded? +
The three interviews are audio-recorded with your written consent. Recordings are transcribed locally on the researchers' hardware, then pseudonymised and the original audio files are deleted.
What language do I respond in? +
English is the working language of the MTnG, so the surveys are administered in English. You're free to respond in whichever language feels most natural, including switching mid-answer if that better captures what you mean.
What do I get out of it? +
At the end of the 10-week cycle, you will receive a personalized report offering direct insights into your AI usage patterns. This structured feedback will help you understand how your cognitive habits and academic workflow have evolved over the term.
How is my data handled? +
Carefully. Download the privacy statement and data notification form below to read the full technical details on your own time.
